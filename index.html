Timothy Holmes

Physicist/Computational Mathematician/Computer Scientist,
and Everything In-Between.

I am a recent graduate of DePaul University with a Bachelor’s Degree in physics and applied mathematics. During my junior year at DePaul University, I joined a research group that studied transparent conducting oxides (TCO) and another research group where we studied thermodynamic properties of semiconductors at the nanoscale. These opportunities allowed for me to work at Argonne National Laboratory with in the Advanced Photon Source (APS). During my time as a research assistant I was able to, collect data, analyze data, and improve my skills in many fields.


I am constantly trying to improve myself by collaborating with other peers and keeping my curiosities open in the research field. My research interest lie in condensed matter physics, quantum computing, numerical analysis topics, machine learning, and big data.
